LEGAL AGENT KNOWLEDGE BASE
============================

--- DOCUMENT 1: ECOA 15 U.S.C. §1691 — Core Prohibition and Adverse Action ---
Source: U.S. Code, Title 15, Chapter 41, Subchapter IV

CORE PROHIBITION:
"It shall be unlawful for any creditor to discriminate against any applicant, 
with respect to any aspect of a credit transaction on the basis of race, color, 
religion, national origin, sex or marital status, or age (provided the applicant 
has the capacity to contract); because all or part of the applicant's income 
derives from any public assistance program; or because the applicant has in good 
faith exercised any right under this chapter."

ADVERSE ACTION NOTICE REQUIREMENT — §1691(d):
"Within thirty days after receipt of a completed application, the creditor shall 
notify the applicant of action taken."

"Each applicant against whom adverse action is taken shall be entitled to a 
statement of reasons for such action from the creditor."

SPECIFICITY REQUIREMENT — §1691(d)(3):
"A statement of reasons meets the requirements of this section ONLY if it 
contains the SPECIFIC reasons for the adverse action taken."

RIGHT TO REQUEST REASONS:
Applicant has the right to request specific reasons within 60 days of receiving 
the denial notice. Creditor must respond within 30 days of that request.

PRIVATE RIGHT OF ACTION — §1691e:
Consumers may bring a civil action for actual damages, punitive damages up to 
$10,000, and attorney's fees. Class actions available for systemic violations.

STATUTE OF LIMITATIONS:
5 years from date of violation for individual actions.


--- DOCUMENT 2: Regulation B — 12 CFR §1002.9 — Adverse Action Procedures ---
Source: eCFR, Title 12, Chapter X, Part 1002, Section 1002.9

TIMING REQUIREMENT:
"A creditor shall notify an applicant of action taken within 30 days after 
receiving a completed application."

CONTENT REQUIREMENTS — WHAT MUST BE IN THE NOTICE:
The notification must contain:
1. A statement of the action taken
2. The name and address of the creditor
3. A statement of ECOA antidiscrimination provisions
4. The name and address of the federal agency that administers compliance
5. SPECIFIC REASONS for the adverse action (or right to request them)

SPECIFICITY STANDARD:
"The statement of reasons must be specific and indicate the principal reason(s) 
for taking adverse action. Creditors should disclose up to four principal reasons."

ALGORITHMIC DECISIONS — NO EXCEPTION:
"Creditors who use complex algorithms, including artificial intelligence or 
machine learning, in any aspect of their credit decisions must still provide 
a notice that discloses the specific principal reasons for taking an adverse 
action." (CFPB Circular 2022-03)

"A creditor's lack of understanding of its own methods is therefore NOT a 
cognizable defense against liability for violating ECOA and Regulation B's 
requirements." (CFPB Circular 2022-03)

DIRECT APPLICATION TO AUTOLEND DENIAL:
AutoLend cited "debt-to-income ratio exceeds acceptable threshold" without 
stating: (a) what the threshold is, (b) what the applicant's ratio was, or 
(c) how the automated system determined the ratio. Under 12 CFR §1002.9 and 
CFPB Circular 2022-03, this is insufficient — the applicant cannot identify 
or correct the issue without these specifics.


--- DOCUMENT 3: CFPB Circular 2022-03 — Algorithmic Adverse Action Notices ---
Source: CFPB Circular 2022-03, consumerfinance.gov/compliance/circulars/
circular-2022-03

KEY HOLDINGS:

"Creditors may not simply use checklist-style forms if they do not reflect 
the actual reason for the denial of credit or other adverse action."

"Whether a creditor is using a sophisticated machine learning algorithm or more 
conventional methods to evaluate an application, the legal requirement is the 
same: the creditor must provide the specific principal reasons for the adverse 
action taken."

"A creditor's lack of understanding of its own methods is NOT a cognizable 
defense against liability."

"While some creditors may rely upon various post-hoc explanation methods, 
such explanations approximate models and creditors must still be able to 
validate the accuracy of those approximations."

CASE CITATIONS IN CIRCULAR:
"Fischl v. Gen. Motors Acceptance Corp., 708 F.2d 143, 146 (5th Cir. 1983) 
— calling the notice requirements 'perhaps the most significant of the 1976 
amendments to the ECOA.'"

"Treadway v. Gateway Chevrolet Oldsmobile Inc., 362 F.3d 971, 977-78 
(7th Cir. 2004) — notice requirement is 'a strong and necessary adjunct 
to the antidiscrimination purpose of the legislation.'"

VIOLATION STANDARD FOR AUTOLEND:
AutoLend's "Automated Decisioning Department" language is precisely the 
scenario this Circular addresses. The institution cannot use algorithmic 
complexity as a shield against providing specific reasons.


--- DOCUMENT 4: FCRA 15 U.S.C. §1681m — Requirements on Users of Consumer Reports ---
Source: U.S. Code, Title 15, §1681m (Section 615)

ADVERSE ACTION NOTICE REQUIREMENTS WHEN CREDIT REPORT IS USED:
"If any person takes any adverse action with respect to any consumer that is 
based in whole or in part on any information contained in a consumer report, 
the person shall:
(1) Provide oral, written, or electronic notice of the adverse action
(2) Provide the name, address, and telephone number of the consumer reporting 
    agency that furnished the report
(3) Provide a statement that the CRA did not make the adverse decision and 
    is unable to explain why the decision was made
(4) Provide notice of the consumer's right to a free copy of the report 
    within 60 days
(5) Provide notice of the consumer's right to dispute the accuracy or 
    completeness of the report"

CREDIT SCORE DISCLOSURE REQUIREMENT (Dodd-Frank Amendment):
When a credit score is used in an adverse action decision, the creditor must 
disclose:
- The numerical credit score
- The range of possible scores
- The key factors that adversely affected the score (up to 4 factors)
- The date the score was created
- The name of the consumer reporting agency that provided the score

DIRECT APPLICATION TO AUTOLEND DENIAL:
AutoLend disclosed the credit score (581, range 300-850, Experian, March 11) — 
this is COMPLIANT. However, the denial does NOT disclose the key factors that 
adversely affected the score as required by the Dodd-Frank amendment to FCRA 
§615. The stated reasons ("insufficient credit history", "DTI", "delinquent 
accounts") may or may not match the actual score factors — if they don't, 
this is a separate FCRA violation.


--- DOCUMENT 5: ECOA Damages and Remedies — 15 U.S.C. §1691e ---
Source: U.S. Code, Title 15, §1691e

PRIVATE RIGHT OF ACTION:
Any applicant who has been subjected to a violation may bring a civil action 
in federal court.

DAMAGES AVAILABLE:
- Actual damages (financial harm caused by the denial)
- Punitive damages up to $10,000 for individual actions
- Class action: lesser of $500,000 or 1% of net worth of creditor
- Attorney's fees and costs (fee-shifting provision)

ADMINISTRATIVE ENFORCEMENT:
CFPB has primary enforcement authority. Can refer to DOJ for pattern and 
practice cases. State attorneys general may bring parallel actions.

STATUTE OF LIMITATIONS:
5 years from date of violation for individual actions. 1 year from 
commencement of DOJ or agency action for related private suits.

FILING OPTIONS:
1. CFPB complaint portal: consumerfinance.gov/complaint
2. Federal court civil action (private right of action)
3. State AG complaint (parallel state enforcement)
4. DOJ Civil Rights Division (pattern and practice referral)


--- DOCUMENT 6: CFPB Circular 2023-03 — Adverse Action and Algorithmic Specificity ---
Source: CFPB Circular 2023-03, consumerfinance.gov/compliance/circulars/
circular-2023-03

UPDATED STANDARD FOR ALGORITHMIC DENIALS:
"Creditors may not simply use checklist-style forms if they do not reflect 
the actual reason for the denial of credit or other adverse action. This 
requirement is especially important with the growth of advanced algorithms 
and personal consumer data in credit underwriting."

"The legal requirement to explain the reasons for adverse actions helps 
improve consumers' chances for future credit and protects consumers from 
illegal discrimination and serves an educational role, allowing consumers 
to understand the reasons for a creditor's action and take steps to improve 
their credit status or rectify mistakes."

SPECIFIC VIOLATION PATTERN:
If the model uses dozens of variables but the adverse action notice lists 
only three generic categories, the notice fails to reflect the actual reasons. 
The creditor must identify which specific model inputs drove the decision — 
not just broad categories.

DISCLOSING CREDIT REPORT USAGE:
"Disclosing that a credit report was obtained and used, as the FCRA requires, 
does NOT satisfy the ECOA requirement to disclose specific reasons." 
(12 CFR 1002.9(b)(2)-9)

This means AutoLend's disclosure of Experian as the credit bureau does not 
substitute for explaining the specific algorithmic decision factors.


--- DOCUMENT 7: Federal Reserve — Top ECOA Violations 2023 ---
Source: Federal Reserve Consumer Compliance Outlook, 2024 (Top Violations 2023)
URL: consumercomplianceoutlook.org/2024/fourth-issue/common-violations-regulation-b/

TOP DOCUMENTED VIOLATIONS IN 2023 EXAMINATIONS:

1. FAILURE TO PROVIDE AAN WITHIN 30 DAYS:
"A common violation was failing to provide an AAN within 30 days after 
receiving a completed application on which adverse action was taken."

2. INSUFFICIENT SPECIFICITY OF REASONS:
"Regulation B requires the statement to be specific and indicate the 
principal reason(s) for taking adverse action. Creditors should disclose 
up to four principal reasons."

"Finding that 'credit references are insufficient' was not an adequate 
statement of reasons because it did not signal the nature of the deficiency." 
(Fischl v. GMAC, 708 F.2d 143)

3. MISSING FEDERAL AGENCY CONTACT INFORMATION:
The adverse action notice must include the name and address of the federal 
agency administering compliance for that creditor type. AutoLend's denial 
letter does not include this — a documented common violation.

4. MISSING APPEAL PROCESS INFORMATION:
While not always legally required, absence of any appeal pathway combined 
with vague reasons has been cited in examination findings as a fair lending 
risk indicator.

DIRECT APPLICATION:
AutoLend's denial contains at least violations 2 and 3 above — insufficient 
specificity and missing federal agency contact. These are the two most 
commonly cited ECOA violations in 2023 examinations.
