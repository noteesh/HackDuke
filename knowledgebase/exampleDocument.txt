DENIAL LETTER FORMAT REFERENCE

FCRA Adverse Action Notice (required for credit denials):
- Must state specific reasons (not just "our system")
- Must include credit score if used (score, range, date, bureau name)
- Must include right to free credit report within 60 days
- Common reason codes: insufficient credit history, high debt-to-income ratio, 
  derogatory marks, insufficient income, too many recent inquiries, 
  length of credit history, high revolving utilization

HUD/FHA Housing Denial Format:
- Must cite specific program criteria not met
- Must include appeal rights and deadline (typically 30 days)
- Often references 24 CFR Part 203

Insurance Claim Denial Format:
- Must cite specific policy exclusion or coverage limitation
- Must include internal appeal process (first level)
- Must include external review rights for health insurance (ACA requirement)
- Often includes claim number and adjuster name

Government Benefits Denial (SNAP, Medicaid, SSI):
- Must cite specific eligibility criterion not met
- Must include fair hearing rights
- Must include deadline for requesting hearing (typically 90 days)
- Will reference specific federal/state regulation

Common Sparse Denial Letters (algorithmic/automated):
- Often only say "did not meet our criteria" or "based on your credit profile"
- May reference a score without explaining thresholds
- Absence of specific reasons is itself legally significant — flag this
